Oemar Said Tjokroaminoto

The Crownless King of Java who forged unity and strength through the formation of Islamic unions

Timeline of Oemar Said Tjokroaminoto's Life

  • August 16, 1882: Born in Ponorogo Regency, East Java, Indonesia, as the second of twelve children.
  • 1902: Graduated from OSVIA (Opleiding School Voor Inlandsche Ambtenaren) and began working as a civil servant in Ngawi.
  • 1905-1907: Moved to Surabaya and met Samanhudi, founder of the Islamic Trade Union.
  • 1905: Samanhudi founded Sarekat Dagang Islam (SDI) in Surakarta; Tjokroaminoto became involved in its management.
  • September 10, 1906: SDI's statutes were prepared and notarized in Surabaya.
  • 1912: At Tjokroaminoto's suggestion, the organization changed its name to Sarekat Islam (SI).
  • 1913: Tjokroaminoto and SI leaders met with Governor-General Idenburg to seek legalization for the organization.
  • 1915: Established Centraal Sarekat Islam (CSI) with Tjokroaminoto as chairman.
  • June 1916: First national congress of CSI held in Bandung under Tjokroaminoto's leadership.
  • 1918: Tjokroaminoto and Abdoel Moeis became members of the Volksraad.
  • 1921: Arrested on charges of assassination; released in August 1922 without trial.
  • February 1923: CSI renamed Partai Sarekat Islam (PSI).
  • 1929: PSI transformed into Partai Syarikat Islam Indonesia (PSII).
  • 1934: Attended the 20th PSII Congress in Banjarmasin.
  • December 17, 1934: Died in Yogyakarta. His brother, Abikusno Tjokrosujoso, succeeded him in PSII.
